A structured starting point for any serious AI-assisted research session. Based on [[Epistemic Hygiene]] — the discipline of knowing how you know what you know.

The prompt:

1. State your research goal in one sentence. What specifically do you want to understand or verify?

2. State what you already believe about this topic.

3. State what you expect or hope to find. This is where motivated reasoning hides — naming it upfront makes it visible rather than invisible.

4. State your current knowledge level. Beginner, intermediate, or experienced changes how output should be framed.

5. Ask the AI to flag uncertainty, contested claims, and the difference between established evidence and hypothesis throughout.

6. Before acting on any key claim, verify it independently from a primary source.

7. At the end of the session ask: what did we assume that wasn't examined? What still needs checking?

A research session without this structure tends toward confirmation of what you already believe. This prompt interrupts that tendency before it starts.
