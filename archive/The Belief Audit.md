A structured self-examination process for surfacing inherited beliefs and evaluating whether they were consciously chosen. Not therapy. Not a substitute for professional support. A tool for deliberate self-directed examination.

Three layers:

Layer 1 — Inventory. What do I believe about myself, others, and the world that I have never consciously examined? These are the beliefs that feel like facts rather than positions.

Layer 2 — Origin. Where did each belief come from? Personal experience, family, culture, media, religion, trauma? Most inherited beliefs have identifiable sources once examined.

Layer 3 — Audit. Is this belief actually mine? Does it serve my current goals and values? Would I choose it consciously today if I were choosing from scratch?

This is the process of auditing your operating system — identifying inherited code that's running in the background, deciding what to keep, what to update, and what to remove entirely.

A belief that survives all three layers is worth keeping. A belief that doesn't is worth examining further —with professional support if it connects to significant trauma.

This process is [[Memnetic]] awareness made practical. [[Epistemic Hygiene]] applied to the self rather than to external information. [[RLHF]] awareness — recognizing which reward functions are running on you and deciding consciously whether to keep them.
