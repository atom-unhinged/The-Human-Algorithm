A structured starting point for AI-assisted research and thinking sessions. Designed to reduce confirmation loops, minimize noise generation, and keep output honest and verifiable from the first exchange.

The problem this solves: most people engage AI reactively — querying without clear intention, accepting output without verification, and generating slop without realizing it. The result is wasted iterations, false confidence, and degraded signal over time.

The framework — apply at the start of any serious AI research session:

1. State your actual goal explicitly. Not "tell me about X" but "I want to understand X in order to do Y."
2. State what you already know and believe — including what you want to be true. Unstated preferences are where confirmation bias lives.
3. State your confidence level on the topic. Expert, intermediate, or beginner changes everything about how output should be framed.
4. Request that the AI flag uncertainty, contested claims, and the difference between established evidence and hypothesis.
5. Commit to verifying key claims independently before acting on them.
6. At the end of the session, ask: what did we assume that we didn't examine? What should be checked?

This framework doesn't guarantee good output. It creates conditions where good output is more likely and bad output is more visible.

[[Epistemic Hygiene]] is the principle. This is the practice.
